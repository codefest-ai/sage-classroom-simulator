About This Artifact

This dashboard is a working instructional decision-support prototype for synchronous online teaching. SAGE is the simulation and formative-evaluation environment: it generates observable-participation proxy signals from 15 agent students, routes them through the dashboard, and records instructor response behavior. SAGE does not measure engagement, attention, or learning outcomes.

What it demonstrates: 15 student agents with distinct behavioral profiles generate observable participation signals in a simulated 45-minute class. The IDSS detects patterns such as participation decline, confusion clusters, equity imbalance, and low-observable-participation conditions, then generates prioritized advisory recommendations. In one-player mode, a human instructor can respond through the inline recommendation cards using a 5-category taxonomy: ignore, acknowledge, accept, modify, reject.

What is currently in scope: Pattern detection uses rule-based computed thresholds and recommendations are rule-based and advisory. University presets supply institutional enrollment context, and each run can produce different outcomes. Participation proxies are formula-driven in all modes; when an LLM backend is available it only enriches simulated student chat dialogue, and does not replace rule-based participation signals or recommendation logic. The instructor role can remain simulated or human-controlled.

Deployment-oriented extension: Zoom meeting events can be connected to the dashboard through webhooks as the intended live deployment path. The current implementation is still prototype-level, so the main Phase 3 evidence lane remains the simulated classroom workflow.

Theoretical grounding: Endsley (1995) situation awareness, Sweller (1988) cognitive load, Hevner et al. (2004) DSR guidelines, Venable et al. (2016) FEDS evaluation framework, Van Leeuwen et al. (2019) advising-oriented learning analytics, and Wise & Jung (2019) instructional decision-making.
